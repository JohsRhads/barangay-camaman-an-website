ABOUT THE BARANGAY

HISTORY

Barangay Camaman-an gets its name from the Cebuano word "maman," which refers to the chewing of betel nut, a widespread tradition among the area's original settlers. Throughout the early 1950s, it wasn't the bustling neighborhood it is today. Instead, it was a quiet, sparsely populated agricultural outskirt of Cagayan de Oro, made up of small sitios with just a handful of farming families. As Cagayan de Oro started booming into a major commercial hub, families began migrating outward from the crowded city center and into Camaman-an. You can see this growth mirrored in the local Catholic community. What started as a tiny 8x12 meter chapel built on donated land in the 1950s had to continually expand, eventually being declared the Our Lady of Fatima Parish in 1989 just to keep up with the swelling population.

VISION

"The strategic and prime development hub of the south, a city managed through good governance, with an empowered citizenry that thrives in a highly competitive economy, and a sustainable environment that nurtures its diversity and multi-cultural heritage towards a resilient, progressive, and inclusive future."

MISSION

"To best serve all stakeholders through the empowerment of the citizenry; humane, efficient, and transparent participatory governance; the delivery of services that respond to stakeholders' needs; compassionate adherence to the rule of law; and the transformation of Cagayan de Oro as the prime city of convergence."

LOCATED

Address: Hayes Street (Extension), Barangay Camaman-an, Cagayan de Oro City, Misamis Oriental, Philippines, 9000. Key Landmark: It sits along the main thoroughfare of the lower, urbanized section of the barangay, located just a short distance from the Our Lady of Fatima Parish Church and the Camaman-an Elementary School.
